Fall Symposium 2025

October 30, 2025
9:30 a.m. –2:30 p.m.
Memorial Union

The Fall Symposium this year is a celebration of those people at URI who “teach outside the box”. The people who help students uncover their love for something new; those who mentor students and help them adjust their path; those who recognize that the most efficient path is not necessarily the straight one. This Symposium celebrates those of you who walk those paths with students.

Our keynote speakers are Scott Carlson and Ned Laff who are the authors of Hacking College: Why the Major Doesn’t Matter—and What Really Does.

Cultivating Vitality Through Intentional Rest

October 22, 2025
3 p.m. –4:15 p.m.
Anchor Room of Higgins Welcome Center

Feeling exhausted, more cynical, or less effective in your work? Burnout may be knocking at your door. Come learn about preventing (or managing) burnout by recognizing the signs, contrasting burnout with vitality-enhancing behaviors, and deepening your understanding of the importance of rest. Leave with a personalized plan for intentional rest and improved well-being.

ATL Resources and Program Spotlight

Teaching Effectiveness Webpage

The primary purpose of this page is to provide guidance to URI instructors seeking to translate their experiences teaching in the classroom into evidence of effective teaching for their portfolios and tenure and promotion packets. Secondly, this page includes information that will help instructors develop an effective teaching statement as well as supporting materials, and provides a roadmap for colleagues to use who desire to partner with ATL as they build their portfolios and align their work in the classroom with the 2025-2028 URI AAUP contract.
Visit our teaching effectiveness webpage to learn more.

Strategies & Tools for New Part-Time Faculty

This program is a great way for new part-time faculty to orient themselves to URI and learn effective course design and teaching strategies so they can put their best foot forward during their first semesters. The program can be experienced asynchronously via Brightspace, as well as through optional individual teaching consultations and small group Zoom sessions.
Visit our website to learn more about the program or reach out to Penny Edwards (penny.edwards@uri.edu), Faculty Development Specialist in ATL.

Difficult Dialogues Webpage

Are you interested in planning ahead to facilitate class discussions that will tackle challenging content to enhance your course objectives and deepen student learning? The purpose of these guides is to provide instructors with concrete strategies that they can use to orient their class towards meeting learning outcomes through difficult discussions rather than to allow them to disrupt learning.
Visit our website for more information.
